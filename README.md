# Round-the-World-Math
Program designed to teach kids arithmetic skills while educating them on languages and countries around the world. 
Made for HACKMESA 2.0 in 4/17/2026 with team "Quantum Lions"

**Background**
Studies show that there are budget cuts happeing in California as student enrollment in k-12 continues to decrease. Students are being taken out of the public education system, and to give direction to their families by creating accessiable and free programs for lesson planning allows them or even educators in classrooms to use tools to help their students learn more effectively. A Stanford study showed third graders who played math video games had built a skill to problem-solve through open-ended math problems. Video games created a pathway for them to learn by using productive regular practice. In our project, our program will be a game that paves the oppurtunity for students to learn arithmatic whilst having fun!

**Objective**
The game aims to teach arithmetic skills to students, while also merging fun facts to stimulate their curiosity and keep them entertainted. The game is on a point system, and students will recieve congradutlatory messages as they answer questions correctly, and encouraging messages when they answer them wrong. 


**Functionality**

User is prompted for their name for personalization.
User selects a language (English, Spanish, French).
The game presents randomized arithmetic questions.
Correct answers show a congratulatory message and a cultural fun fact in the selected language.
Incorrect answers show an encouraging message.
Final score summary is shown after all questions.
