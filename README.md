# Round-the-World-Math

Program designed to teach kids arithmetic skills while providing them with fun facts from around the world. 
Made for HACKMESA 2.0 on 4/17/2026 with team "Quantum Lions"

**Background**

Data from the California Department of Education and EdSource reveals a concerning trend: budget cuts across California are intensifying as K–12 student enrollment continues to decline. As more students leave the public education system, families and educators are left searching for effective, accessible resources to support learning. This creates an urgent need for free, engaging educational tools that can bridge the gap and empower both students and teachers.

Research supports this approach. A Stanford study found that third-grade students who engaged with math-based video games developed stronger problem-solving skills, particularly when working through open-ended mathematical challenges. These games fostered consistent, productive practice—an essential component of meaningful learning.

**Objective**

The game aims to teach arithmetic skills to students, while also merging fun facts to stimulate their curiosity and keep them entertainted. The game is on a point system, and students will recieve congradutlatory messages as they answer questions correctly, and encouraging messages when they answer them wrong. 


**Functionality**

"Round the World Math" is an interactive, bilingual (English and Spanish) math quiz game where users select a power-up and answer ten randomized arithmetic questions. As the player progresses, they earn points based on their performance and the chosen power-up, while also being rewarded with a "fun fact" in their selected language after every correct answer. Once the ten rounds are finished, the program displays the final score and saves both the player's name and result to a scores.txt file.
